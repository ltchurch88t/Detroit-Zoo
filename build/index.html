Welcome to the Detroit Zoo

Mother's Day brunch

Celebrate Mother’s Day at the Detroit Zoo! This year’s Mother’s Day Brunch will be held in the Main Picnic Grove and features a delicious brunch buffet and a gift for mom. A Bloody Mary and mimosa cash bar will be open from noon to 2 p.m. Walk-up tickets will be available if the event is not sold out; however, advance ticket purchase is recommended. Pre-sold tickets will include admission to the Zoo; walk-up tickets will not.

Free
Meet Your Best Friend

Meet Your Best Friend at the Zoo is one of the nation’s largest off-site pet adoption events. The Detroit Zoo hosts this semiannual event in partnership with the Michigan Humane Society. Since the event’s inception in 1993, more than 25,000 dogs, cats and rabbits have been placed into new homes at the spring and fall events. Meet Your Best Friend is a free event and takes place in the parking lot beneath the Zoo’s landmark water tower.

Dinosauria

Forty lifelike animatronic dinosaurs that snarl and move – and some that spit – will provide a mega-dose of Vitamin Z. Dinosauria is the largest dinosaur exhibit of its kind in the country. Enter a veritable “Zoorassic World” as you travel back in time along a lush, winding, 3-acre DinoTrail recreating prehistoric life. Enormous primeval creatures lurk at every turn. Open daily at 9:30 a.m. starting May 25. Tickets are $6

Scout Camps

Meet badge requirements by visiting the Detroit Zoo! Bring your scout group and choose one of our daytime adventures. All programs include a tour of the Zoo and an interactive biofact presentation by one of our knowledgeable volunteer educators. General quests are available for all ages, as well as select specific badges for Cub Scouts and Girl Scouts.

Family Programs

From Family Fun Fridays to Africa Adventure Stories to Zoo Tots, you’ll be amazed at all the Zoo has to offer as you and your family as you heighten your sense of wonder through stories, activities and experiences.

Camps

Summer Safari Camp Join us for a mega-dose of Vitamin Z at Summer Safari Camp 2018! A variety of incredible activities are in store during the summer break at the wildest place in town. Spaces are limited and go quickly! Children must be registered for camp levels corresponding to the school grade they will enter

Humane Education

The Berman Academy for Humane Education was created to help people help animals and is located in Detroit Zoo’s Ford Education Center. Learn more about how the Academy offers a broad range of unique and engaging programs designed to meet the needs of our diverse audiences.

Conservation

The care and rescue of animals that ultimately live at the Detroit Zoo and Belle Isle Nature Center. Their stories help to enhance conservation knowledge and action by the more than 1.6 million people who visit these facilities each year. Many animals under our care are also part of critical assurance populations to guard against extinction. Numerous field research and conservation projects with amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals and butterflies to study and conserve wild populations. Support of other conservation organizations in the field as well as related Detroit Zoological Society education initiatives. Response to environmental emergencies where our specially trained staff rescue and rehabilitate wildlife affected by such disasters.
